Israeli Drivers in Japan: The Legal Guide to Driving Permits

Renting a campervan in Japan after your military service or for a family vacation? Learn how to legally obtain your 1949 Geneva Convention IDP in Israel before your departure.

✍️
VANTRIPJAPAN Team
5 min read

Renting a campervan and road-tripping through Japan is one of the most popular ways to celebrate completing your military service (Shichrur) or experience a unique holiday. However, before you pack your bags and head to Japan, you must ensure you have the correct driving documents. Without them, campervan rental companies like VanTripJapan legally cannot hand over your keys.

Driving without a valid license in Japan is a serious offense. Under Japanese law, using an invalid permit is categorized as unlicensed driving (無免許運転). This carries severe penalties, including immediate arrest, heavy fines, potential deportation, and the automatic invalidation of all insurance coverage.

To avoid any issues at vehicle pickup, here is the exact guide to getting a legally recognized International Driving Permit (IDP) in Israel.

1. The Geneva Convention Requirement (1949)

Unlike many countries that accept various types of international permits, Japan is extremely strict. Japan is only a party to the 1949 Geneva Convention on Road Traffic. Therefore, Japan only recognizes IDPs issued under this specific 1949 convention.

The permit must be a physical grey paper booklet. The following are not valid in Japan:

  • Digital or electronic permits on your phone
  • Plastic card-style international licenses
  • IDPs issued under the 1968 Vienna Convention

2. ⚠️ Alert: Avoid Private Online Driving Permits

Many travelers try to purchase "International Driving Licenses" online from private websites, such as the International Automobile Authority (IAA) or similar organizations. These companies print translations and card-style permits.

Please note: Private online permits (such as IAA) are completely illegal for driving in Japan.

The Japanese police and official rental companies do not recognize private translation companies or unauthorized online sellers. Do not waste your money on these websites — you will be refused your rental vehicle and cannot drive in Japan.

3. Where and How to Get a Legal IDP in Israel

Fortunately, getting a valid IDP in Israel is quick and cheap, but it must be done before you leave the country. You cannot apply for one once you arrive in Japan or through the Israeli Embassy in Tokyo.

Step 1: Authorized Issuers

The Israeli Ministry of Transport authorizes specific bodies to print official 1949 IDP booklets on behalf of the government. The primary issuer is MEMSI (The Automobile & Touring Club of Israel).

Official booklets can also be obtained at designated photography and optics shops throughout Israel, including select branches of Optica Halperin and Meravnet.

Step 2: Bring the Correct Documents

When you visit a MEMSI branch or authorized station, make sure to bring:

  • Your valid Israeli plastic driver's license (temporary paper licenses are not accepted)
  • Your Israeli ID (Teudat Zehut) or Passport
  • The printing fee (approximately 15 to 20 NIS)
The booklet is printed on the spot and takes less than 10 minutes.

Step 3: Check the Cover Page

Before leaving the desk, look at the front cover of the booklet. It must state: "Convention on Road Traffic of 19 September 1949". If it has this convention name, it is valid in Japan.

4. Picking Up Your Campervan in Japan

When you arrive at the VanTripJapan pickup counter, you must present the original physical copies of three documents. If any of these are missing, we cannot legally hand over the vehicle:

  1. Your valid Israeli plastic driver's license
  2. Your valid 1949 Geneva Convention IDP booklet (issued in Israel)
  3. Your Passport (to verify your entry date and tourist status)
We cannot accept photocopies, scans, or photos of these documents.

5. Frequently Asked Questions

"I am already outside of Israel. What can I do?"

If you have already left Israel, you cannot get an IDP online. However, a family member or friend in Israel can obtain one for you. They will need to bring a clear copy of your Israeli driver's license and a letter of authorization signed by you to a MEMSI branch. Once they receive the physical booklet, they can send it to your hotel or campervan pickup location in Japan via express courier (like EMS or DHL).

"How long can I drive with my IDP in Japan?"

An IDP is valid for one year from its date of issue, or for one year from your date of arrival in Japan (as stamped in your passport), whichever period is shorter. If you plan to stay in Japan longer than one year, you must convert your license to a Japanese one.

Summary

A campervan road trip is the ultimate way to explore Japan's stunning mountains, coastlines, and hidden hot springs. Taking 10 minutes to visit an authorized MEMSI office before you depart will ensure your trip is safe, legal, and entirely stress-free. Have a wonderful adventure!

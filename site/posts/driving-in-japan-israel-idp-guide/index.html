How to Legally Drive in Japan for Israeli Travelers: The Essential IDP Guide

Planning a campervan road trip in Japan after your military service or for a holiday? Here is everything you need to know to get a legally valid International Driving Permit (IDP) in Israel before leaving.

✍️
VANTRIPJAPAN Team
5 min read

Planning to rent a campervan for a road trip around Kyushu or elsewhere in Japan? Doing a road trip after finishing your military service (Shichrur) is a dream trip. But before you book your flights and dream about the open road, there is one critical thing you must get right: your International Driving Permit (IDP).

Japan is notoriously strict about driving licenses. If you arrive in Japan with the wrong document, your rental company cannot hand over the keys — it is illegal. Even worse, driving with an invalid permit is treated as unlicensed driving (無免許運転) under Japanese law, which can lead to arrest, deportation, and invalidation of your insurance.

Here is your essential guide to ensuring your IDP is 100% legal in Japan before you leave Israel.

1. The Golden Rule: Only the 1949 Geneva Convention is Valid

There are different types of International Driving Permits in the world. Japan only recognizes IDPs issued under the 1949 Geneva Convention on Road Traffic.

It must be a physical booklet. Digital permits, plastic card-style permits, or booklets under the 1968 Vienna Convention are strictly invalid in Japan.

2. ⚠️ WARNING: Online-Bought Permits (like IAA) are NOT Valid

Many travelers fall into the trap of purchasing "International Driving Licenses" online from private companies. The most common one is the International Automobile Authority (IAA).

Documents from the IAA are NOT valid in Japan.

The Japanese police and rental car agencies do not recognize private translation agencies. Attempting to use an IAA permit in Japan is illegal. Please do not waste your money on these websites — they will not allow you to drive in Japan.

3. How to Get a Valid IDP in Israel (Step-by-Step)

Since Israel is a signatory to the 1949 Geneva Convention, you can easily get a legal IDP. You must obtain it before you depart for Japan. You cannot get it at the Israeli Embassy in Tokyo or inside Japan.

Step 1: Locate an Authorized Issuer in Israel

The Ministry of Transport in Israel authorizes specific organizations to issue official IDPs. The most famous one is MEMSI (Automobile & Touring Club of Israel).

You can also get them at authorized optical photography stations across Israel, such as chains like Optica Halperin and Meravnet.

Step 2: Bring the Required Documents

Go to an authorized branch and bring:

  • Your valid permanent plastic Israeli driver's license
  • Your Israeli ID (Teudat Zehut) or Passport
  • A small fee (typically around 15–20 NIS)

Step 3: Confirm the 1949 Convention Stamp

The issuer will print a paper booklet for you on the spot. Double-check the cover to ensure it states: "Convention on Road Traffic of 19 September 1949". This is the magic phrase that makes it valid in Japan.

4. What You Must Carry While Driving in Japan

When you pick up your campervan and whenever you are on the road in Japan, you must carry these three original documents at all times:

  1. Your valid Israeli plastic driver's license
  2. Your valid 1949 Geneva Convention IDP booklet (issued by MEMSI/authorized station)
  3. Your Passport (the rental agency must verify your Japanese entry stamp date)
Photocopies or digital photos on your phone are not accepted.

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

"I have already left Israel. What can I do?"

If you are already traveling in another country, you cannot apply for an IDP online or in Japan. However, you can authorize a family member or close friend in Israel to go to a MEMSI branch on your behalf. They will need a copy of your driver's license and a letter of authorization. Once they obtain the physical booklet, they can mail it to your hotel or campervan pickup location in Japan via express courier (like DHL or EMS).

"How long is the IDP valid in Japan?"

An IDP is valid for one year from the date of issue, AND you can drive in Japan for up to one year from your date of entry (stamped in your passport), whichever comes first.

Enjoy a Safe and Legal Road Trip!

Exploring Japan in a campervan is one of the most rewarding ways to see the country's hidden gems, mountain peaks, and coastal villages. By spending just 10 minutes at a MEMSI branch before you leave home, you can guarantee a smooth, legal, and worry-free adventure of a lifetime.

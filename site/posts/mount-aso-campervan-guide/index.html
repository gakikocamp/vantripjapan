Mount Aso by Campervan: Volcano, Views & Van Life

✍️
VANTRIPJAPAN Team
7 min read

Mount Aso is one of the most dramatic landscapes in Japan — a massive volcanic caldera stretching 25km across, with an active smoking crater at its center. It's one of the best places in Kyushu for campervan travel.

Why Mount Aso Is Perfect for Campervans

The caldera is essentially a giant natural amphitheater with rolling grasslands, farms, and villages inside it. There are multiple campsites with views you simply can't get from a hotel room — waking up inside a volcano is an experience unique to van life.

What to See and Do

Aso Nakadake Crater

The active crater is accessible by car (when volcanic activity permits). Drive up the Aso Panorama Line to the crater rim parking lot. The sulfurous smoke and turquoise crater lake are otherworldly. Check the alert level before visiting — Level 1 means open, Level 2+ means closed.

Kusasenri Grassland

A vast, flat grassland at 1,100m altitude with a shallow pond at its center. Horseback riding available. The sunset views here are among the best in Kyushu.

Daikanbo Viewpoint

The best panoramic view of the entire caldera. On clear days, you can see all five peaks of Aso and the towns inside the caldera. Free parking.

Aso Shrine

One of Japan's oldest shrines, recently rebuilt after the 2016 earthquake. The weekly morning market on the approach is excellent for local produce.

Where to Camp

  • Bozu Jigoku Campground — Free campsite near Beppu with basic facilities
  • Aso Kuju National Park camps — Several campsites with volcanic views
  • Michi-no-Eki Aso — Roadside station with parking, shops, and restaurants

⚠️ Volcanic Safety

Mount Aso is an active volcano. Check the Japan Meteorological Agency alert level before visiting. Level 1 is normal; Level 2+ restricts access to the crater. Gas masks are available at the crater parking lot.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to visit Mount Aso?

Yes, when the alert level is at Level 1 (Normal). The area is well-monitored. Access to the crater is immediately restricted when activity increases. The caldera towns are safe regardless of alert level.

How long should I spend at Mount Aso?

One full day is enough for the crater, viewpoints, and grassland. Two days allows for hiking and hot springs. The onsen at the foot of the volcano are some of the best in Kyushu.
